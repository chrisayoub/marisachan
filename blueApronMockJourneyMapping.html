Blue Apron: Mock Journey Mapping

OVERVIEW

The Project

Blue Apron is a service that provides customers a box filled with pre-packaged ingredients and deliverers it to their door.

My Role: UX Researcher

BARRIERS

The Problem

I wanted to better understand how a customer's emotional journey through every stage of interacting with the company and how it competes in the market as a boxed-product.

OUR SOLUTION

Create an emotional customer journey map of Blue Apron.

PROCESS

Brainstorm & Research

The novelty of delivering a company product via box is a modern marketing tactic

PROCESS

Execution

I decided to gather a group of test participants based on convenience sampling due to the general usability of the app and because it did not require any prior knowledge of the company to interact with our MVP.

I sat down with each participant and started each interaction with the first MVP open on a mobile device and had them complete simple navigation tasks involving the iconography set. Although I had them verbalize their thought processes with each decision they made, I also took notes on their non-verbal behaviors and errors as well. I had participants follow this process a total of three times for each prototype and compare each experience.

RESULTS

Feedback

We found that there was an overall favorability towards the second prototype due to its intuitive design and simplicity in getting its purpose across. However, throughout testing, 100% of participants made the same error while attempting to complete the same task. Although I was not testing information navigation, I took note that there may be an issue in the current information architecture of our app.

REFLECTIONS

Next Steps

Based on the feedback I collected, I presented my data to the team and we have since implemented the best received iconography set into our mobile application. In addition to the feedback, I was able to find other problems users ran into while interacting with our app. I plan to use that information and create another research plan to take a look at our current information architecture format and look at ways to reorganize the information so it is more intuitive for users to find and retest.
