InTouch

OVERVIEW

The Project

InTouch was designed and developed at a 24-hour hackathon, and is a health and wellness application that helps users track their mental and physical activity.

My Role: UX Designer, UI Designer, Graphic Designer
Collaborators: Chris Ayoub, John Cauvin, Niklas Lauffer

BARRIERS

The Problem

College students are regularly stressed out and fall behind on their personal well-being. We explored a way for students to manage their health with the touch of a finger.

OUR SOLUTION

Create a mobile and wearable app that allows for students to take mental and physical breaks while concurrently monitoring their dietary health.

PROCESS

Brainstorming

We began our process by jotting down common stressors people experience in their daily lives and ways to consolidate those characteristics into a single app. As a team, we decided both a mobile and wearable app was the best direction to take, because it would allow users to sync their daily health data from the watch to the app as well as have access to data from their phone. Afterwards, I sketched up a simple bare-bones design of our app and discussed it with our team's developers.

PROCESS

Wireframing

After improving and finalizing the sketches, I created lo-fidelty wireframes using Sketch.

PROCESS

Design: The Watch Application

The watch allows users to physically connect with our app and helps guide them through features, such as the meditation timer. It provides users with a personal experience and enhances their interaction, because they do not have to be connected with the phone to use the app.

PROCESS

Design: The Mobile Application

Our accompanying mobile application allows users to decide which health aspect they would like to focus on and engage in the appropriate sets of activities. The app offers activities in the categories of Mind, Body, and Health. Mind provides the opportunity to complete guided meditation of the user's desired length. Body offers various relaxing exercises that the user can do without any complicated gym equipment. Finally, Health lets the user input various lifestyle data, such as diet and habits, so that we can visualize correlations of this data with the user's meditation and exercise.

REFLECTIONS

My Thoughts

A problem we immediately ran into during the hackathon was deciding which features to allocate our time towards. Within our 24-hours, we were able to build a functional mobile application but were unable to develop a working wearable app. Despite this, we hope to take our idea and look into building a working wearable application like we initially intended.
