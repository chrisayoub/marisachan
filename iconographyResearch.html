Iconography Research

OVERVIEW

The Project

Gardenio's mobile application includes an iconography set to help users navigate and use the app. I was tasked with exploring other icon options to implement in place of the current icon pack.

My Role: UX Researcher, UX Research Designer, Graphic Designer

BARRIERS

The Problem

Given the feedback we received from user-testing, I took note of recurring statements regarding the iconography from the MVP interface. The main pain point expressed by users was the unintuitive and confusing icons on the bottom navigation bar which toggled between the "Home" page and the "Care Guides" page. I worked to explore other possible iconography sets to make navigation more usable for future users.

OUR SOLUTION

Conduct a qualitative, comparative usability test with other icon sets to measure possible increases in icon recognition through in-context testing.

PROCESS

Research Design

Our tight budget meant I had to be efficient in gathering new icon sets. We ran into a barrier after deciding our team could not afford to purchase new sets at the time. Luckily, I was able to design and create three separate icon sets to test in-context while keeping our users concerns in mind. Using InVision, I implemented the icons into the app interface and created three separate prototypes for each icon set to run comparative tests.

PROCESS

Execution

I decided to gather a group of test participants based on convenience sampling due to the existing usability of the app and because it did not require any prior knowledge of the company to interact with our MVP.

I sat down with each participant and started each interaction with the first MVP open on a mobile device, and had them complete simple navigation tasks involving the iconography set. Although I had them verbalize their thought processes with each decision they made, I also took notes on their non-verbal behaviors and errors as well. I had participants follow this process a total of three times for each prototype and verbally compare each experience.

RESULTS

Feedback

We found that there was an overall favorability towards the second prototype due to its intuitive design and simplicity in getting its purpose across. However, throughout testing, 100% of participants made the same error while attempting to complete the same task. Although I was not testing information navigation, I took note that there may be an issue in the current information architecture of our app.

REFLECTIONS

Next Steps

Based on the feedback I collected, I presented my data to the team, and we have since implemented the best received iconography set into our mobile application. In addition to the feedback, I was able to find other problems users ran into while interacting with our app. I plan to use that information and create another research plan to take a look at our current information architecture format and look at ways to reorganize the information so it is more intuitive for users to find and retest.
