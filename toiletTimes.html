Toilet Times

OVERVIEW

The Project

Toilet Times is an Android app that allows students to anonymously rate and comment about campus bathrooms.

My Roles: UI Designer, Graphic Designer, Branding
Collaborators: Mustafa Abban, Chris Ayoub, Anthony Liu, David Huang, Clare Zhang

BARRIERS

The Problem

College students want to know the best locations to use the restroom across campus, but tracking down a clean stall with minimal traffic is often challenging. Within the 24 hour competition time crunch, we had to be as creative and efficient as possible to create something that does just that.

OUR SOLUTION

Develop a fully functional app that acts as a student review board for the toilet stalls and urinals across campus.

Link to Devpost project page

PROCESS

Brainstorming

We began our process by collectively brainstorming and asking other students what features of a restroom are important to them. Due to the competition's time constraint, we were unable to achieve a representative sample of university students, but rather a large sample of computer science students. However, our sample still proved to be sufficient for the project. From our list of items, we determined its level of importance and decided which to implement based on our limited time frame. I sketched a rough wireframe to visualize general page flow and possible mappings.

PROCESS

Design & Implementation

Following the ideation process, I created lo-fi wireframes which showcased elements of the application in more detail using Adobe Illustrator. I synced up with our group developers and discussed the features we could implement with our tight schedule.

PROCESS

Branding

For branding, we felt it was important for our app to have a fun and playful color scheme. I decided to pick a bright blue and complementing dark blue for the app's relationship with water.

APPLICATION

The App

We wanted our application to populate reviews of restrooms and the buildings they are in by anonymous users, and allow them to "up-vote" or "down-vote" them in a simple manner. We also wanted our users to be able to look at the most recent comments and browse the most "up-voted" of all time by making those easily accessible through a menu.

REFLECTIONS

My Thoughts

Our short period with developing this application taught me how to be efficient while on a time crunch. I also had the opportunity to team up with developers and fully engage myself in not only the design aspect of creating an app, but the technical side as well. We hope to later implement more features into the app, such as looking at past posts a user has made and populating those in one location.
